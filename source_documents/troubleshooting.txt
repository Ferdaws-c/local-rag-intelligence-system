SmartHome Hub v2.0 — Troubleshooting Guide

Problem: Hub LED is Blinking Red Constantly
A constantly blinking red LED indicates the device has encountered a critical firmware error. Do not unplug the device immediately. Wait 5 minutes to allow any background process to complete. If the LED continues to blink red after 5 minutes, perform a factory reset by holding the power button for 10 seconds. After the reset, reinstall the latest firmware through the SmartHome App before re-adding your devices.

Problem: Smart Devices Are Not Responding to Commands
If your smart devices stop responding, first check whether the hub's LED is solid white (normal operation). If the LED is normal but devices are unresponsive, open the SmartHome App and check the device status. If the app shows the device as offline, try toggling the device's physical power switch off and on. If the device remains offline, delete it from the app and re-pair it following the standard pairing procedure.

Problem: Hub is Offline in the App When Away from Home
Remote access requires the hub to maintain an outbound connection to the SmartHome cloud service. If the hub appears offline in the app when you are away, check whether your home internet connection is active. Log into your router remotely or ask someone at home to confirm the router is functioning. A power outage or ISP outage at home will cause the hub to appear offline in remote sessions.

Problem: Automations and Schedules Are Not Running
If scheduled automations stop running, first check the system clock in the hub settings via the SmartHome App. An incorrect system time caused by a power outage or NTP failure can prevent time-based automations from triggering. Reconnecting the hub to the internet will automatically resync the clock. Also verify that the automation is still enabled in the app and that the conditions and triggers are correctly configured.

Problem: Hub Runs Hot and Shuts Down
If the hub shuts down unexpectedly and is hot to the touch, it has triggered its thermal protection mechanism. Move the device to a cooler location with better airflow. Do not stack objects on top of the hub. The operating temperature range is 0°C to 40°C. If the device continues to overheat in a well-ventilated location, contact support as this may indicate a hardware defect covered under warranty.
